## 1. English Report: A Global Gallop

The past week has delivered an exhilarating mix of record-breaking performances, major G1 clashes, and intense build-up to upcoming festivals, underscoring horse racing's global appeal and constant excitement.

**The Cheltenham Countdown Dominates UK & Ireland:**
The anticipation for the Cheltenham Festival is palpable, with a significant number of headlines dedicated to the prestigious event. The biggest talking point remains **Constitution Hill**, with owner Michael Buckley expressing a desire for a Flat campaign targeting the Melbourne Cup, a notion echoed by Gordon Elliott who urged him to "stay on the Flat." However, his Champion Hurdle participation is still pending a crucial schooling session, leaving fans and pundits in suspense. Trainer quotes abound, with Willie Mullins declaring his "biggest and strongest Cheltenham team by a country mile," and Nicky Henderson "playing a blinder" with his Kempton runners. Stable tours, ultimate guides, and expert tips for races like the Turners' and County Hurdle are fueling the excitement, while concerns over Triumph Hurdle favourite Narciso Has being ruled out add to the drama.

**Hong Kong Sensation Ka Ying Rising Makes History:**
Globally, the standout performance came from **Ka Ying Rising** in Hong Kong, who captured headlines across multiple publications for achieving an astounding 18th consecutive win, breaking a local record. Trainer and jockey comments consistently praise his development and dominance, solidifying his status as a true racing marvel.

**Japanese G1 Action Heats Up:**
Japan hosted major G1 action with the **February Stakes**, where **Costanova** secured a historic consecutive victory, delighting connections and drawing praise from jockey Christophe Lemaire. Despite strong competition from Double Heart Bond and Wilson Tesoro (who picked up his 7th "silver medal" in G1s), Costanova's prowess was undeniable. Elsewhere, **Sonshi** made a spectacular return after a year-long absence to win the Hankyu Cup in course-record time, astonishing jockey Yuga Kawada. The Diamond Stakes saw **Stinger Glass** claim his first graded victory, with Lemaire touting his long-distance aptitude for future challenges like the Tenno Sho (Spring). The Kokura Daishoten delivered a memorable moment with **Tagano Dude's** first graded win, coinciding with jockey **Yoshihiro Furukawa** achieving his 600th JRA career victory. Other significant jockey milestones included **Yuichi Kitamura** reaching 1000 JRA wins and **Kiwamu Ogino** hitting 300 wins, showcasing the depth of talent in the Japanese riding ranks. The 500 million yen horse **Sagarmata** also secured his maiden win, generating significant buzz.

**International Highlights and Industry Trends:**
The international circuit saw **Forever Young** stun the field to become the first dual Saudi Cup winner, with his owner already eyeing a hat-trick. **Outrange** accepted an invitation to the Dubai World Cup. In Australia, **Tropicus** landed his maiden Group 1 in the Oakleigh Plate, while **Streisand** delivered a dominant Blue Diamond victory. The Magic Millions Perth Yearling Sale reported "strong trade" with record-breaking figures, including Coolmore's reported $70 million purchase of stallion **Super Seth**. North American Derby preps are gaining momentum, with **Great White** winning the Battaglia and **Jack's Promise** taking the Gulfstream Park Sprint.

Beyond the track, discussions are ongoing regarding governance and funding challenges within the industry, with Winfried Engelbrecht-Bresges warning that global racing is "under threat" and Peter Savill questioning Lord Allen's credibility amidst a governance standoff. Yet, the overall sentiment, as expressed in one headline, points to racing having "a lot of growth potential." The betting landscape remains a prominent feature, with various tipsters, analytical series like "Masters of Betting," and discussions around market dynamics. Tragically, Group 1 winner Reliable Man and Black Caviar's sire Bel Esprit both passed away at 18 and 26 respectively.

In summary, the horse racing world is buzzing with major events, captivating narratives, and significant achievements, from Cheltenham's looming grandeur to Hong Kong's rising star and Japan's competitive G1 scene.
