### 3. Industry Governance, Regulation & Infrastructure

**Facts:**
Significant structural and regulatory changes are underway. TABcorp and Racing NSW announced an agreement to introduce a National Tote in Australia, aiming to "future-proof racing" (ID 89, TTR AusNZ). This comes with SuperTAB punters facing higher takeout rates (ID 75, The Straight). The Queensland Racing Integrity Commission (QRIC) is facing internal turmoil, with deputy commissioner Kim Kelly resigning over concerns that forthcoming organizational changes will compromise QRIC's independence from Racing Queensland (ID 83, TTR AusNZ). The Breeders' Cup announced a new foal nomination structure (ID 58, BloodHorse News) and reelected board members (ID 66, BloodHorse News). The Jockey Club launched a new Tattoo ID feature (ID 50, BloodHorse News). Horse travel is now permitted from South Africa to Britain (ID 51, BloodHorse News). The future of racing at Hawthorne is in question, with no takers to keep it going, leaving shutdown questions to the court (ID 30, 74, BloodHorse News). Monmouth Park's future could be boosted by surrounding development (ID 54, BloodHorse News). The IFHA held a Climate Change and Sustainability Summit (ID 68, BloodHorse News).

**Quirky Details:**
The detailed allegations by Kim Kelly regarding QRIC's independence highlight a specific internal governance challenge within Australian racing (ID 83, TTR AusNZ).

**Quotes:**

**Facts:**
Concerns regarding horse welfare, safety, and environmental factors are emerging. Several horses have been sidelined due to injury, such as Reef Runner for the rest of 2026 (ID 29, BloodHorse News) and Bentornato with a sesamoid injury, now out of training (ID 45, 103, BloodHorse News, DRF News). The International Federation of Horseracing Authorities (IFHA) held a Climate Change and Sustainability Summit (ID 68, BloodHorse News), indicating a growing industry focus on environmental responsibility.
