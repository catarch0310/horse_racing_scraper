### 1.1. Affordability Checks & Betting Industry Impact (UK)

**Facts:**
The implementation of affordability checks by the Gambling Commission in the UK has been a dominant and contentious issue. Bookmakers report significant customer loss to the black market due to these checks, which are described as "absolutely disgusting" (ID 9, Bookies, racing_post). The policy has been given the green light despite strong opposition from both racing bodies and bookmakers (ID 23, racing_post). MPs have expressed "huge concern" over the lack of control regarding the policy (ID 21, MPs, racing_post). Punters face "worse odds, black market risk and inconsistency" (ID 22, racing_post). The UK Tote Group has reported an £8.5m loss in a "challenging" environment (ID 20, UK Tote Group, racing_post), which could be exacerbated by these changes. Despite this, a minister has backed industry growth and refused to bow to advertising pressure, offering a "rare win for gambling" (ID 52, racing_post). The sport's reliance on betting is explicitly acknowledged, with "key racing figures" discussing its place (ID 53, racing_post).
